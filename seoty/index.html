National Student Employment Week- April 13 - April 19, 2014

 

Each year Auburn University celebrates National Student Employee Appreciation Week, during the second full week of April. While part-time jobs offer students career enhancing opportunities, students as employees make substantial contributions to the operation of the University and the local community. The Career Center encourages Auburn Univeristy departments, colleges and offices as well as Auburn/Opelika area businesses to celebrate student employee contributions during Student Employee Appreciation Week. Student Employee Appreciation Week enables us to thank Auburn students for the contributions they make as employees to the University and the surrounding community.

Student Employee Appreciation Week Events

Student Employee of the Year - 2014

During National Student Employment Week festivities, the Student Employee of the Year Recognition Ceremony will honor two outstanding student employees selected from nominated candidates, one employed on-campus and one employed in the community.

Eligibility
  • Must be a current Auburn University student in good academic standing enrolled for the Spring 2014 semester.
  • Must have worked three (3) months full-time or six (6) months part-time between July 1, 2013 and May 31, 2014 (anticipated) for a total of at least 240 working hours during this time period.
  • May include undergraduate or graduate students who are on student employment payroll (this does include Resident Assistants).
  • Graduate, Research, or Teaching Assistants are NOT eligible.
Nominations

To nominate a student, complete the online nomination form by Friday, February 7, 2014. Only one student nomination per nominator. Complete nominations include the following:

  • REQUIRED: On-Campus Student Employee Nomination Form (Complete and submit online)
    OR
  • REQUIRED: Off-Campus Student Employee Nomination Form (Complete and submit online)
  • OPTIONAL: Letter of Nomination - 2 pg limit (Only ONE letter will be considered for each nominee)
  • Tips for a Great Nomination Letter (PDF) (Letter must be submitted via online nomination form as noted above.)
Selection Criteria

An independent selection committee comprised of University staff members and community employers will rate the following qualities for each nominee:

  • Reliability
  • Initiative
  • Quality of Work
  • Professionalism
  • Uniqueness of Contribution
Process

Two (2) winners will be chosen by the selection committee; one employed on-campus and one employed in the Auburn/Opelika community. The recipient of the on-campus award is eligible to compete for the regional Student Employee of the Year (SASEA). The regional winner will compete for the title of National Student Employee of the Year. Regional and National winners receive cash awards.

     

Support Opportunities

National Student Employment Week (NSEW) presents a unique and rare opportunity for your business to have an on-campus marketing presence. All support will be recognized on the event website and in all marketing materials. You can be a sponsor in one of the following ways:

  • Offering a discount or special promotion during the month of April to Auburn University student employees who present a pay stub and a Auburn student ID. Examples: $5 off a meal, free pastry with purchase of coffee drink, 10% off any purchase, reduced admission prices, buy one get one free, etc.
  • Provide an item for the Student Employee of the Year winners' (qty: 2) giftbags. Suggested items include: gift cards, gift baskets, t-shirts, tickets, professional portfolios, etc.
  • Provide promotional items to be handed out to students during the week or to be placed in Student Employee of the Year nominees' goodie bags (approx: 25-40). Suggestions items include: pens, cups, coozies, t-shirts, coupons, etc.

For more information please visit the Supporter Information Page or contact Jay Skipworth at 334-844-3864.
