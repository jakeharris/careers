Career Center > Experience > Spotlight - Cameron

This is my experience...

Cameron Johnson

- Summer 2011 internship with NASA at Johnson Space Center

- May 2013 graduate in finance

See More Spotlights! Intern Spotlight Archive
Tell us about yourself

I'm a senior from Dothan, AL. I am a member of the College of Business Executive Society and actively involved in Alternative Student Breaks. I also want to work abroad.

Where did you intern and how did you find this opportunity?

The summer of 2011 I interned with NASA at Johnson Space Center as a Contract Specialist in Houston, TX. I've always been interested in our space program and applied directly through their portal, but I do believe I saw information about it on TRL after I already had applied.

Describe your internship experience and the responsibilities you had.

As a Contract Specialist intern, I supported the Flight Crew Operations Directorate, Astronaut's office, and Safety & Mission Assurance. I completed multiple phases of the contract and purchase order process, including solicitation, synopsis, and the awarding phase. I also performed incremental funding modifications to several contracts that required additonal funds in order to complete specifications. Finally, another major task of mine was conducting variance analyses on existing contracts in order to re-evaluate fund allocation and progess of objectives. Aside from my job responsibilites, it was a historic time to work for NASA then because  my internship was during the final launch of the Space Shuttle Program. It was great experience to work, but it was also amazing to tour Mission Control, the Shuttle simulator and see every thing else, inculding traveling to Florida to see the last Shuttle launch in-person.

Have you participated in any other internships during your college career?

I interned at EBSCO Industries last summer (2012). I worked as a Product Management Intern for Mozaic, a new service that allows subscribers to read magazines and periodicals electronically.

Do you recommend that other Auburn students participate in internships and why or why not?

I strongly recommend it because of the invaluable experience you gain. Aside from the work you perform, it's so important to get a feel for what work is really like once you graduate. It is much easier to obtain a full-time position if you have internship experience. It's also beneficial because you can figure out what field might or might not interest you . While on-campus leadership involvement is important, internship experience will differentiate you when you begin interviewing for full-time employment and it stands out more to employers.

What are your career plans following graduation?

I have accepted a position with Regions Financial Corporation as a participant in their Finance Development Program.

What advice do you have for other Auburn students looking to gain experience while in college?

Don't be afraid to apply for any internship position. I was a sophomore when I applied for NASA and I didn't think I was qualified, but I was one of only 8 interns selected and of one only two sophomores. The worst that can happen is you're rejected. Apply for any thing you might be interested in!
