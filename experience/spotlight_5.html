Career Center > Experience > Spotlight - Eddie

This is my experience...

Eduardo (Eddie) Robison Rivera

Internship: Spring 2013 with The Walt Disney Company

Title: Industrial Engineering Professional Intern

Major: Industrial and Systems Engineering

Class Year: Junior

Hometown: Pleasanton, CA

Campus Activities: IIE Member, Founding member of the Lee County Flannel Club Improv Troupe

Hobbies: Improv, Video Games, Reading

See More Spotlights! Intern Spotlight Archive
How did you find your internship?

I found my internship online through the Disney interns website. I already had an idea of the types of internships that were offered, but I went to the website to apply for those that were specific to my major. I also completed a Disney College Program before my internship with the Industrial Engineering Department. It allowed me to work at Disney, take an engineering course, and network with individuals who were industrial engineers.

Describe your internship experience and the responsibilities you had.

My internship experience was something that is difficult to describe in words—because it was beyond my expectations! As a professional intern with Industrial Engineering, I was placed under the role of supporting Attractions across the Walt Disney World Property. Since my coach (or mentor) was the lead industrial engineer for Magic Kingdom attractions, I ended up focusing a great deal of my time there.

I was responsible for various projects dealing with efficiency, capacity analysis, and guest counting accuracy. Under the supervision of my coach, I was given projects which I could lead myself. It was my responsibility to create a project plan, coordinate with clients, execute a study using available resources, analyze the data, and present the project to the clients. This allowed for the true experience of what it was like to be working as an industrial engineer.

Also, during spring break season (one of the busiest seasons for theme parks) I was responsible for providing efficiency tips to various attractions. I would visit a few attractions each day during their peak periods, and analyze what was done well in the operation of the attraction, and where improvement might be found.

What did you enjoy most about your internship?

The freedom, responsibilities, and learning opportunities are what I enjoyed most about my internship. I was given the freedom to manage my own time and organize my day via an outlook calendar. This allowed me to physically see my schedule and manage my time and resources accordingly. I was also given responsibility for delivering in all aspects of a project. I enjoyed being able to see a project from start to finish, and know that it was my project—we were encouraged to take ownership for our work. Finally, Disney was a big proponent of ongoing learning. I had the opportunity to attend different informational meetings or to go to Disney University to learn additional information that might be helpful in my field.

What was the most difficult aspect of your internship?

The most difficult aspect of my internship was communicating effectively and concisely. I tend to be a talkative person, so I would tend to have wordy e-mails when I could have more effectively displayed the information in a table or in a few sentences. Communication is a skill which I feel is underestimated, but it’s always crucial. An individual in Industrial Engineering must be able to communicate at all levels—from the frontlines up to executives.

How did your experience at Auburn University prepare you for your internship?

Auburn prepared me for my internship by allowing me the resources that I needed in order to be successful. Not only were a number of my courses relevant to the work I was doing, but the interactions I had with the Career Center allowed me to be better prepared for what to expect, and how to ask the right questions.

How did your internship prepare you for a career?

Honestly, my internship allowed me to sort of “test drive” my real life. I was living in a condo, had a commute to work, and worked a regular “8-5” job. That in and out of itself was an experience. The internship itself exposed me to the types of tools, resources, and knowledge required to be successful in my field. I have a greater understanding of what it means to be an industrial engineer, and what the expectations are of a potential employer. In general, I feel significantly more prepared for a career in industrial engineering after my internship.

What advice do you have for other Auburn students looking to gain experience while in college?

As funny as it may sound, I would recommend that students go for their dreams. Once you find something that you are passionate about, use all of the resources that Auburn makes available in order to go about achieving your dream. Initially, I applied for the industrial engineering internship and the college program at the same time. I did not get the IE internship, but I decided to use my college program as a learning tool and a networking opportunity. That, mixed with many visits to the career center, gave me the leg up when I again applied for the IE internship.

Even though Disney was always my 1st choice, I went to the Career Expo and met with other employers and got myself out there. The best thing you can do for yourself is to open as many doors as possible.
What are your career plans following graduation?

I hope to return for another internship with Disney during Summer 2014, at which point I will be able to interview for a full-time job there. It would be a dream come true to work full-time with the Industrial Engineering Department at Disney; however, like I said, I’ll be sure to keep as many doors open as possible.
