Service Experience

Auburn University offers a comprehensive variety of service opportunities including service learning courses, one-time outreach events, international study-abroad service programs and more.  These initiatives enrich the undergraduate learning experience by providing students in-depth exposure to societal issues.  Students also learn civic responsibility and are able to make a meaningful impact in the local, regional, national and/or international community. Service locations may include community service agencies, non-profit organizations, educational institutions or health care settings.

Center for Community Service

Connects students with community organizations and opportunities to volunteer on campus, in the local community, across the nation and around the world.

Center for Community Service Student Center Suite 3130 | 334.844.4788 | Email

Opportunities Include:  
Alternative Student Breaks Students perform community service during academic breaks.
AU Dance Marathon A year-long campus wide fundraising effort benefitting Children's Miracle Network Hospitals.
Beat Bama Food Drive An annual fundraising drive benefiting the East Alabama Food Bank.
Campus Food Pantry An initiative to aid Auburn University students struggling with food insecurity.
Global Service Initiative Provides assistance to students who are looking for service opportunities domestically and internationally.
IMPACT Auburn's central resource for volunteer opportunities and community service in the Auburn community.
