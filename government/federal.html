Federal Pay

Unlike salaries for most private sector jobs, salaries for most federal civil service jobs are set on the General Schedule, or GS pay scale, which maps required experience and level of job responsibility against a system of grades and steps within each grade.

Grade Qualifying Education
GS-5 4 academic years beyond high school leading to a bachelor's degree OR a bachelor's degree
GS-7

Bachelor's degree with one of the following:

- Class Standing (upper third of class)
- 3.0 or higher GPA; 3.5 or higher GPA in major
- Honor society membership
GS-9 Master's degree (or equivalent)
GS-11 Doctorate Degree

 

For additional information on the GS scale visit:

  • Office of Personnel Management (OPM) Standards
  • General Schedule (GS) Chart
  • Partnership for Public Service (PPS) Federal Pay Info
Federal Resume

USAJobs.gov offers a Resume Builder to help you create a resume that will provide the information government agencies need when making hiring decisions. This resume builder allows your to create one resume that will satisfy the requirements of many agencies.

Federal Job Search Websites

USAJobs.gov is a good place to begin your federal job search. Most government agencies are required by law to post any vacancies open to outside applicants on this website. However, not all government jobs will be posted on this website to so it is also important to visit the website of those agencies with which you are interested in working.
